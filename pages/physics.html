1. Mechanics

1.1 Kinematics

Equations of Motion (uniform acceleration)

v = u + at
s = ut + (1/2)at²
v² = u² + 2as
s_n = u + a(2n - 1)/2   [distance in nth second]

Projectile Motion

  • Horizontal displacement: x = u cosθ × t
  • Vertical displacement: y = u sinθ × t - (1/2)gt²
  • Time of flight: T = 2u sinθ / g
  • Maximum height: H = u² sin²θ / 2g
  • Range: R = u² sin 2θ / g
  • Maximum range (at θ=45°): R_max = u² / g
  • Trajectory equation: y = x tanθ - gx² / (2u²cos²θ)

Circular Motion

  • Angular velocity: ω = dθ/dt = v/r
  • Angular acceleration: α = dω/dt
  • Relation: v = rω, a_tangential = rα
  • Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r = rω²
  • Centripetal force: F_c = mv²/r = mrω²
  • Time period: T = 2π/ω = 2πr/v
  • Frequency: f = 1/T = ω/(2π)

1.2 Newton's Laws & Friction

  • First Law (Inertia): A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net force
  • Second Law: F_net = ma (vector equation)
  • Third Law: F_AB = -F_BA (action-reaction, equal and opposite)
  • Weight: W = mg (g = 9.8 m/s² or 10 m/s² in JEE)
  • Impulse: J = F × Δt = Δp = m(v - u)
  • Static friction: f_s ≤ μ_s N
  • Kinetic friction: f_k = μ_k N (μ_k < μ_s always)
  • Angle of friction: tan φ = μ
  • Angle of repose: tan θ = μ_s
  • Pseudo force (non-inertial): F_pseudo = -ma_frame

1.3 Work, Energy & Power

  • Work: W = F · s · cosθ = F⃗ · s⃗ (dot product)
  • Kinetic Energy: KE = (1/2)mv²
  • Work-Energy Theorem: W_net = ΔKE = KE_f - KE_i
  • Gravitational PE: PE = mgh
  • Spring PE: PE = (1/2)kx²
  • Conservation of Energy: KE + PE = constant (conservative forces only)
  • Power: P = W/t = F · v · cosθ
  • Efficiency: η = (useful output / total input) × 100%

  • Parallel Axis Theorem: I = I_cm + Md²
  • Perpendicular Axis (lamina): I_z = I_x + I_y
  • Torque: τ = r × F = Iα
  • Angular momentum: L = Iω = r × p

  • Rolling without slipping: v = rω

1.5 Gravitation

  • Newton's Law of Gravitation: F = Gm&sub1;m&sub2;/r²
  • G (gravitational constant): 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
  • Gravitational field g: g = GM/r²
  • Surface gravity (Earth): g_0 = GM/R² = 9.8 m/s²
  • g at height h: g_h = g_0 R²/(R+h)² ≈ g_0(1 - 2h/R) for h << R
  • g at depth d: g_d = g_0(1 - d/R)
  • Gravitational PE: U = -GMm/r
  • Orbital velocity: v_0 = √(GM/r) = √(gR²/r)
  • Orbital velocity (surface): v_0 = √(gR) ≈ 7.9 km/s
  • Escape velocity: v_e = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR) ≈ 11.2 km/s
  • v_e = √2 × v_0 (surface): escape = √2 times orbital

Kepler's Laws

  • First Law: Planets orbit in ellipses with the Sun at one focus
  • Second Law: Equal areas swept in equal times (conservation of angular momentum)
  • Third Law: T² ∝ r³   (T²/r³ = constant for all planets around the same star)

2. Properties of Matter & Fluid Mechanics

2.1 Elasticity

  • Stress: Force / Area (Pa = N/m²)
  • Strain: Change in dimension / Original dimension (dimensionless)
  • Young's Modulus Y: Y = (F/A) / (ΔL/L) = FL / (AΔL)
  • Bulk Modulus B: B = -V ΔP / ΔV = ΔP / (-ΔV/V)
  • Shear Modulus G: G = (F/A) / (shear strain)
  • Poisson's Ratio ν: ν = -(lateral strain) / (longitudinal strain), -1 < ν < 0.5

2.2 Surface Tension & Capillarity

  • Surface tension T: T = F/l (force per unit length)
  • Excess pressure (bubble): ΔP = 4T/r (two surfaces)
  • Excess pressure (drop): ΔP = 2T/r (one surface)
  • Capillary rise: h = 2T cosθ / (rρg)
  • Work done to split: W = T × ΔA

2.3 Viscosity & Fluid Flow

  • Viscous force (Stokes): F = 6πηrv (sphere of radius r, velocity v)
  • Terminal velocity: v_T = 2r²(ρ - σ)g / (9η)
  • Reynolds number: Re = ρvd/η (laminar <1000, turbulent >2000)
  • Continuity equation: A&sub1;v&sub1; = A&sub2;v&sub2; (incompressible flow)
  • Bernoulli's equation: P + (1/2)ρv² + ρgh = constant
  • Torricelli's theorem: v = √(2gh) (speed of efflux)

3. Thermodynamics

3.1 Kinetic Theory of Gases

Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT = NkT
R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)    k = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K
N_A = 6.022 × 10²³ /mol (Avogadro's number)
PV = nRT, where n = N/N_A
  • Average KE per molecule: KE = (3/2)kT
  • rms speed v_rms: √(3RT/M) = √(3kT/m)
  • Average speed v_avg: √(8RT/πM)
  • Most probable speed v_mp: √(2RT/M)
  • Speed relation: v_mp < v_avg < v_rms
  • Degrees of freedom f: Monatomic = 3, Diatomic = 5, Polyatomic = 6
  • Internal energy U: U = (f/2)nRT
  • C_V = (f/2)R: Monatomic 3R/2, Diatomic 5R/2
  • C_P = C_V + R: From Mayer's relation
  • Ratio γ = C_P/C_V: Monatomic 5/3, Diatomic 7/5

3.3 Thermodynamic Processes

ProcessConditionWork Done WΔU
IsothermalΔT = 0nRT ln(V&sub2;/V&sub1;)0
AdiabaticΔQ = 0, PV² = const(P&sub1;V&sub1; - P&sub2;V&sub2;)/(γ-1)-W

  • First Law of Thermodynamics: ΔU = Q - W (heat absorbed minus work done BY system)
  • Adiabatic relation: PV² = constant, where γ = C_P/C_V
  • Also adiabatic: TV^(γ-1) = constant
  • Carnot efficiency: η = 1 - T_L/T_H = 1 - Q_L/Q_H
  • Carnot: max efficiency: No real engine can exceed Carnot efficiency
  • C_P - C_V = R: Mayer's relation (for ideal gas)
  • Entropy change: ΔS = Q_reversible / T

3.4 Thermal Expansion & Heat Transfer

  • Linear expansion: ΔL = LαΔT
  • Area expansion: ΔA = 2AαΔT
  • Volume expansion: ΔV = 3VαΔT = VβΔT (β = 3α)
  • Specific heat capacity: Q = mcΔT
  • Latent heat: Q = mL (phase change at constant T)
  • Thermal conduction: Q/t = kA(T&sub1;-T&sub2;)/d (Fourier's law)
  • Stefan-Boltzmann: P = εσAT&sup4; (radiated power)
  • σ (Stefan's constant): 5.67 × 10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K&sup4;)
  • Newton's Law of Cooling: dT/dt = -k(T - T&sub0;)
  • Wien's Displacement Law: λ_max T = 2.898 × 10⁻³ m·K

4. Waves & Sound

4.1 Wave Basics

Wave equation: y = A sin(kx - ωt + φ)
v = fλ = ω/k
ω = 2πf = 2π/T    k = 2π/λ
  • Speed of sound in gas: v = √(γP/ρ) = √(γRT/M)
  • Speed of sound in solid: v = √(Y/ρ) (Y = Young's modulus)
  • Intensity: I ∝ A², I = P/(4πr²) (point source)
  • Intensity level (decibel): β = 10 log(I/I&sub0;), I&sub0; = 10⁻¹² W/m²

4.2 Superposition & Standing Waves

  • Constructive interference: Path diff. Δ = nλ, n = 0,1,2,...
  • Destructive interference: Δ = (2n-1)λ/2
  • Beats frequency: f_beat = |f&sub1; - f&sub2;|
  • Vibrating string (fundamental): f&sub1; = (1/2L)√(T/μ)
  • String harmonics: f_n = n × f&sub1; = n/(2L) × √(T/μ)
  • Open pipe (harmonics): f_n = nv/(2L), n = 1,2,3,...
  • Closed pipe (harmonics): f_n = (2n-1)v/(4L), n = 1,2,3,...
  • Note: Closed pipe has only odd harmonics

4.3 Doppler Effect

f' = f × (v ± v_observer) / (v ∓ v_source)
Upper signs: observer moving toward source / source moving away
Lower signs: observer moving away / source moving toward

5. Electrostatics

5.1 Coulomb's Law & Electric Field

  • Coulomb's Law: F = kq&sub1;q&sub2;/r², k = 1/(4πε&sub0;) = 9×10&sup9; N·m²/C²
  • Permittivity of free space ε&sub0;: 8.854 × 10⁻¹² C²/(N·m²)
  • Electric field: E = F/q = kQ/r²
  • Superposition: E_total = ΣE_i (vector sum)
  • Electric flux: Φ = E · A cosθ
  • Gauss's Law: Φ_total = Q_enc / ε&sub0;

Electric Fields of Common Geometries

5.2 Electric Potential & Energy

  • Potential: V = kQ/r (point charge)
  • Work done: W = q(V&sub1; - V&sub2;) = qΔV
  • Potential energy: U = kq&sub1;q&sub2;/r
  • Equipotential surfaces: Perpendicular to E field; W=0 for charge moving along them
  • Inside conductor: E = 0; entire conductor at same potential
  • Relation E and V: E = -dV/dr

5.3 Capacitors

  • Capacitance: C = Q/V
  • Parallel plate (air): C = ε&sub0;A/d
  • Parallel plate (dielectric): C = kε&sub0;A/d, k = dielectric constant
  • Series combination: 1/C_eq = 1/C&sub1; + 1/C&sub2; + ... (same Q)
  • Parallel combination: C_eq = C&sub1; + C&sub2; + ... (same V)
  • Energy stored: U = (1/2)CV² = Q²/(2C) = QV/2
  • Energy density: u = (1/2)ε&sub0;E²

6. Current Electricity

6.1 Basic Concepts

  • Current: I = dQ/dt (A = C/s)
  • Current density: J = I/A = σE
  • Ohm's Law: V = IR, J = σE
  • Resistance: R = V/I = ρL/A
  • Resistivity ρ: Property of material; units: Ω·m
  • Conductivity σ: σ = 1/ρ
  • Temp. dependence: ρ = ρ&sub0;(1 + αΔT)
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Energy: E = Pt = VIt = I²Rt

6.2 Circuits

  • Series resistors: R_eq = R&sub1;+R&sub2;+... (same current I)
  • Parallel resistors: 1/R_eq = 1/R&sub1;+1/R&sub2;+... (same voltage V)
  • Two parallel resistors: R_eq = R&sub1;R&sub2; / (R&sub1;+R&sub2;)
  • EMF and internal resistance: V_terminal = ε - Ir
  • KCL (junction rule): ΣI_in = ΣI_out
  • KVL (loop rule): ΣV around any closed loop = 0
  • Wheatstone bridge balanced: R&sub1;/R&sub2; = R&sub3;/R&sub4;
  • Potentiometer (comparison): V&sub1;/V&sub2; = l&sub1;/l&sub2;

6.3 RC Circuits

  • Charging capacitor: q(t) = Q&sub0;(1 - e^(-t/τ)), V_C = V&sub0;(1 - e^(-t/τ))
  • Discharging capacitor: q(t) = Q&sub0; e^(-t/τ)
  • Time constant: τ = RC
  • At t = τ: Capacitor charges to 63% (or discharges to 37%)

7. Magnetism, EMI & AC Circuits

7.1 Magnetic Force

  • Force on moving charge: F = qv × B, |F| = qvB sinθ
  • Force on current-carrying wire: F = BIL sinθ
  • Radius of circular path: r = mv/(qB)
  • Time period (cyclotron): T = 2πm/(qB) (independent of speed)
  • Torque on current loop: τ = nBIA sinθ = MB sinθ
  • Magnetic moment: M = nIA

7.2 Biot-Savart Law & Ampere's Law

  • Biot-Savart: dB = (μ&sub0;/4π) I dL sinθ / r²
  • μ&sub0; (permeability): 4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A
  • Center of circular loop: B = μ&sub0;I / (2R)
  • Long straight wire: B = μ&sub0;I / (2πr)
  • Solenoid (inside): B = μ&sub0;nI (n = turns/meter)
  • Toroid: B = μ&sub0;nI (inside only)
  • Ampere's Law: ∮ B · dl = μ&sub0;I_enc
  • Force between wires: F/L = μ&sub0;I&sub1;I&sub2; / (2πd) (attractive if same direction)

7.3 Electromagnetic Induction

  • Faraday's Law: EMF = -dΦ/dt (EMF = -N dΦ/dt for N turns)
  • Magnetic flux: Φ = B · A cosθ
  • Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes the change in flux
  • Motional EMF: EMF = Bvl (rod moving in field)
  • Self-inductance: EMF = -L dI/dt
  • L of solenoid: L = μ&sub0;n²V = μ&sub0;n²Al
  • Mutual inductance: EMF&sub2; = -M dI&sub1;/dt
  • Energy in inductor: U = (1/2)LI²

7.4 Transformers & AC Circuits

  • Ideal transformer: V&sub1;/V&sub2; = N&sub1;/N&sub2; = I&sub2;/I&sub1;
  • Inductive reactance: X_L = ωL = 2πfL
  • Capacitive reactance: X_C = 1/(ωC) = 1/(2πfC)
  • Impedance: Z = √[R² + (X_L - X_C)²]
  • Phase angle: tanφ = (X_L - X_C)/R
  • Resonance frequency: f&sub0; = 1/(2π√(LC))
  • At resonance: Z = R (minimum), I = V/R (maximum)
  • Quality factor Q: Q = ω&sub0;L/R = 1/(ω&sub0;CR)
  • Average power: P_avg = V_rms I_rms cosφ
  • RL circuit (time constant): τ = L/R

8. Optics

8.1 Reflection (Mirrors)

  • Laws of reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection
  • Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f = 2/R
  • Focal length: f = R/2
  • Magnification m: m = -v/u = h'/h
  • Sign convention: Distances measured from pole; incident direction is +ve

8.2 Refraction & Lenses

  • Snell's Law: n&sub1; sinθ&sub1; = n&sub2; sinθ&sub2;
  • Refractive index: n = c/v (c = 3×10&sup8; m/s)
  • Lens formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f
  • Lens maker's equation: 1/f = (n-1)(1/R&sub1; - 1/R&sub2;)
  • Power of lens: P = 1/f (f in metres), unit = Dioptre (D)
  • Lenses in contact: P = P&sub1; + P&sub2; + ..., 1/f = 1/f&sub1; + 1/f&sub2; + ...
  • Magnification (lens): m = v/u = h'/h

8.3 Total Internal Reflection

  • Critical angle C: sin C = n&sub2;/n&sub1; (n&sub1; > n&sub2;)
  • TIR condition: θ > C and light goes from denser to rarer medium
  • Applications: Optical fiber, diamond brilliance, mirage

8.4 Prism

  • Deviation δ: δ = (i&sub1; + i&sub2;) - A
  • At minimum deviation D: n = sin[(A+D)/2] / sin(A/2)
  • Thin prism: δ = (n-1)A
  • Angular dispersion: δ_V - δ_R = (n_V - n_R)A

8.5 Wave Optics

  • Young's Double Slit (bright fringe): y_n = nλD/d
  • Fringe width: β = λD/d
  • Dark fringe: y_n = (2n-1)λD/(2d)
  • Single slit (1st dark): a sinθ = λ
  • Resolving power: θ_min = 1.22λ/d
  • Malus's Law (polarization): I = I&sub0; cos²θ
  • Brewster's angle: tan i_B = n (polarized reflected ray)

9. Modern Physics

9.1 Photoelectric Effect

  • Einstein's photoelectric eq.: KE_max = hf - φ = eV&sub0;
  • Planck's constant h: 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s = 4.136 × 10⁻¹⁵ eV·s
  • Threshold frequency: f&sub0; = φ/h
  • Work function φ: Minimum energy to eject an electron
  • No effect below: f < f&sub0; regardless of intensity
  • Energy of photon: E = hf = hc/λ

9.2 de Broglie Wavelength

  • de Broglie wavelength: λ = h/p = h/(mv)
  • Electron accelerated through V: λ = 1.22/√V nm
  • Heisenberg Uncertainty: Δx · Δp ≥ h/(4π)

9.3 Bohr Model of Hydrogen Atom

Orbit radius: r_n = n² a&sub0; / Z,   a&sub0; = 0.529 Å (Bohr radius)
Energy: E_n = -13.6 Z² / n² eV
Speed: v_n = v&sub0; Z/n,   v&sub0; = 2.18 × 10&sup6; m/s
Angular momentum: L_n = n h/(2π) = nℏ
  • Condition for orbit: 2πr = nλ (standing wave)
  • Photon energy (transition): E = E_higher - E_lower = hf
  • Rydberg formula: 1/λ = R_H Z²(1/n&sub1;² - 1/n&sub2;²)
  • R_H (Rydberg constant): 1.097 × 10&sup7; m⁻¹

9.4 Nuclear Physics

  • Mass defect: Δm = Z m_p + N m_n - M_nucleus
  • Binding energy: BE = Δm × 931.5 MeV/u
  • E = mc²: 1 amu = 931.5 MeV
  • Alpha decay: ᴼXᴸ → ᴸ⁻⁴Yᴼ⁻² + ⁴He²
  • Beta minus decay: n → p + e⁻ + anti-neutrino
  • Beta plus decay: p → n + e⁺ + neutrino
  • Gamma decay: No change in A or Z; energy released as γ photon
  • Radioactive decay law: N(t) = N&sub0; e^(-λt)
  • Activity A: A(t) = A&sub0; e^(-λt) = λN
  • Half-life T½: T½ = 0.693/λ
  • After n half-lives: N = N&sub0; (1/2)^n = N&sub0; (1/2)^(t/T½)
  • Mean life τ: τ = 1/λ = T½/0.693

JEE Advanced Physics Focus Areas

JEE Advanced tests conceptual depth, not just formula application. Focus on: rotational mechanics (complex situations), electromagnetism (multi-concept problems), optics (interference + diffraction), and modern physics (Bohr model + nuclear).

Common Physics Mistakes in JEE

  • Forgetting to use correct sign convention in mirror/lens formula
  • Confusing g variation with height versus depth
  • Wrong direction for magnetic force (use right-hand rule carefully)
  • Forgetting that KE is lost in inelastic collisions
  • Confusing n (turns per meter for solenoid) with N (total turns)
  • Using wrong formula for half-life: N = N_0 (1/2)^(t/T½) not N = N_0 (1/2)^t

13. Solved Practice Problems

Problem 1 — Kinematics: Projectile Range
A ball is thrown at 45° with initial speed 20 m/s. Find the maximum range (take g = 10 m/s²).
Given: u = 20 m/s, θ = 45°, g = 10 m/s² Formula for range: R = u² sin2θ / g sin(2 × 45°) = sin 90° = 1 R = (20)² × 1 / 10 = 400 / 10 = 40 m Maximum range occurs at θ = 45°: R_max = u²/g = 400/10 = 40 m
Range = 40 m
Problem 2 — Work-Energy Theorem
A 2 kg block starts from rest and reaches a speed of 6 m/s. What is the net work done on the block?
Given: m = 2 kg, u = 0 (rest), v = 6 m/s By Work-Energy Theorem: W_net = ΔKE = KE_final - KE_initial = (1/2)mv² - (1/2)mu² = (1/2)(2)(6)² - 0 = (1)(36) = 36 J
Net work done = 36 J
Problem 3 — Capacitors: Energy Stored
A parallel plate capacitor has capacitance 10 μF and is charged to 200 V. Find the energy stored.
Given: C = 10 μF = 10 × 10⁻⁶ F, V = 200 V Energy stored in capacitor: U = (1/2)CV² = (1/2) × (10 × 10⁻⁶) × (200)² = (1/2) × (10 × 10⁻⁶) × 40000 = (1/2) × 0.4 = 0.2 J
Energy stored = 0.2 J = 200 mJ
Problem 4 — Modern Physics: Bohr Model
Find the energy of an electron in the third orbit (n=3) of hydrogen atom.
For hydrogen atom (Z = 1): E_n = -13.6 Z² / n² eV For n = 3, Z = 1: E_3 = -13.6 × (1)² / (3)² = -13.6 / 9 = -1.51 eV The negative sign indicates the electron is bound to the nucleus. The ionization energy from n=3 is +1.51 eV.
E_3 = -1.51 eV
Problem 5 — Nuclear Physics: Radioactive Decay
The half-life of a radioactive substance is 10 years. After how many years will only 1/8 of the original amount remain?
Given: T½ = 10 years, N/N₀ = 1/8 Using: N = N₀ × (1/2)^(t/T½) 1/8 = (1/2)^(t/10) (1/2)³ = (1/2)^(t/10) 3 = t/10 t = 30 years Alternatively: 1/8 = (1/2)³, so 3 half-lives are needed. t = 3 × T½ = 3 × 10 = 30 years
30 years (3 half-lives)

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important physics chapters for JEE?
The most important chapters for JEE by weightage are: Mechanics (Kinematics, Laws of Motion, WEP, Rotational) at 25–30%, Electrostatics & Current Electricity at 18–22%, Modern Physics at 12–15%, Optics at 8–12%, and Magnetism/EMI at 10–14%. Start with Kinematics as it forms the foundation for all other mechanics topics.
What is Newton's second law of motion?
Newton's second law states F = ma. The net external force on an object equals the product of its mass and acceleration. Both force and acceleration are vectors — they point in the same direction. The law implies that a larger force produces a larger acceleration, and a heavier object needs more force for the same acceleration. For variable mass systems, F = dp/dt (rate of change of momentum).
How to remember all physics formulas for JEE?
Strategy: (1) Understand derivations — a derived formula is never forgotten. (2) Create a chapter-wise formula sheet and revise weekly. (3) Practice numericals daily — formulas get reinforced through use. (4) Use dimensional analysis to check or recall formulas. (5) Group related formulas: all mirror/lens formulas use 1/v + 1/u = 1/f (similar structure). (6) Make mnemonics for constants like R = 8.314 (“Eight Three One Four”).
What is the escape velocity from Earth?
Escape velocity from Earth is approximately 11.2 km/s. Derivation: Set KE = gravitational PE, so (1/2)mv² = GMm/R, giving v_e = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR). Substituting g = 9.8 m/s² and R = 6.4×10&sup6; m gives 11.2 km/s. Note: v_e = √2 times the orbital velocity at Earth's surface.
What is the de Broglie wavelength and why is it important for JEE?
The de Broglie wavelength λ = h/p = h/(mv) describes the wave nature of matter. For an electron accelerated through voltage V, λ = 1.22/√V nm (numerically useful for JEE problems). It is important because it underlies Bohr's quantization (nλ = 2πr), electron microscopes, and the wave-particle duality central to quantum mechanics. JEE frequently asks calculations involving this formula and the photoelectric effect together.
