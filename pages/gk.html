Indian Polity — Key Constitutional Facts

  • Constitution adopted: November 26, 1949 (Constitution Day)
  • Constitution effective: January 26, 1950 (Republic Day)
  • Chief Drafter: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (Chairman of Drafting Committee)
  • Total Parts: 25 (originally 22)
  • Total Schedules: 12
  • Original Articles: 395 articles (now 448 due to amendments)
  • Fundamental Rights: Articles 12–35 (Six categories; Right to Property removed as FR in 1978)
  • Directive Principles: Articles 36–51 (non-justiciable but fundamental to governance)
  • Fundamental Duties: Added by 42nd Amendment (1976); Article 51A; 11 duties
  • Emergency provisions: Three types — National Emergency (Art. 352), President's Rule (Art. 356), Financial Emergency (Art. 360)
  • Amendment procedure: Article 368

Important Economic Terms and Definitions

  • GDP (Gross Domestic Product): Total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders in a given period. The most widely used measure of economic size.
  • GNP (Gross National Product): Total value of goods and services produced by a country's residents, regardless of location (includes income earned abroad, excludes income earned by foreigners domestically).
  • NNP (Net National Product): GNP minus depreciation (capital consumption allowance).
  • Inflation: Sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services; reduces purchasing power. Measured by CPI (Consumer Price Index) in India (RBI target: 4% +/- 2%).
  • Deflation: Sustained decrease in general price levels; often indicates economic contraction. Can be more damaging than moderate inflation.
  • Stagflation: Simultaneous high inflation + high unemployment + stagnant economic growth. Rare but severe condition; occurred in 1970s USA due to oil shock.
  • FDI (Foreign Direct Investment): Investment by a company in a foreign country through establishing business operations or acquiring business assets. Long-term investment with management control.
  • FPI (Foreign Portfolio Investment): Investment in financial assets (stocks, bonds) of a foreign country without direct management control. More volatile than FDI; sensitive to market conditions.
  • Balance of Payments (BoP): Record of all economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world. Comprises Current Account, Capital Account, and Financial Account.
  • Monetary Policy: Central bank actions (in India: RBI) to control money supply and interest rates to achieve economic objectives. Tools: repo rate, CRR, SLR.
  • Fiscal Policy: Government's use of taxation and spending to influence the economy. Expansionary fiscal policy: increase spending/reduce taxes to stimulate growth. Contractionary: opposite.
  • Repo Rate: Rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks. Increasing repo rate reduces money supply (fights inflation); decreasing it stimulates borrowing and growth.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

How should I prepare General Knowledge for UPSC Prelims?

For UPSC Prelims GK preparation: (1) Read NCERT textbooks from Class 6 to 12 for History, Geography, Economy, and Science as the foundation. (2) Read The Hindu or Indian Express daily for current affairs and note down important events in a dedicated notebook. (3) Use Vision IAS or Insights IAS monthly current affairs compilations for structured revision. (4) Focus on Indian Polity through M. Laxmikanth's book. (5) Practice previous 10 years' UPSC Prelims papers to understand question patterns. GK for UPSC is primarily static (history, polity, geography, science) combined with current affairs from the last 18 months.

Which newspaper is best for current affairs preparation in India?

The Hindu is the most widely recommended newspaper for UPSC and competitive exam current affairs preparation in India. It provides comprehensive national and international coverage, in-depth editorials, and reliable factual information. Indian Express is the second-best option, especially for political coverage and investigative journalism. For SSC and banking exams, The Hindu or any national English daily supplemented with a monthly GK magazine (Pratiyogita Darpan, Competition Success Review) is effective. Read the editorial section daily as it tests analytical thinking required in UPSC Mains answer writing.

What are the most important GK topics for SSC CGL?

For SSC CGL General Awareness, the highest-scoring topics are: (1) History — Indian Freedom Struggle, major battles, Mughal and British periods. (2) Geography — Indian states, capitals, rivers, physical geography. (3) Polity — Constitutional provisions, Parliament structure, President and PM powers. (4) Science — Physics (electricity, optics), Chemistry (periodic table, reactions), Biology (human body, diseases). (5) Current Affairs — Last 6 months' major events, awards, sports. (6) Economics — Basic concepts like GDP, inflation, RBI, budget terms. (7) Computers — Basic hardware/software, MS Office, internet terms. Static GK typically accounts for 60% and current affairs 40% of SSC General Awareness questions.

How many states and Union Territories does India have?

India currently has 28 States and 8 Union Territories. The count changed in 2019 when Jammu and Kashmir was bifurcated into two Union Territories: Jammu and Kashmir (UT with legislature) and Ladakh (UT without legislature). Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu were merged into a single UT in 2020, reducing the UT count from 9 to 8. The 8 UTs are: Delhi (NCT), Puducherry, Jammu and Kashmir, Ladakh, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu, and Chandigarh.

Who is the current Secretary-General of the United Nations?

Antonio Guterres (Portugal) is the 9th Secretary-General of the United Nations. He assumed office on January 1, 2017, and was re-appointed for a second term starting January 1, 2022, through December 31, 2026. Before becoming UN Secretary-General, he served as Prime Minister of Portugal (1995-2002) and as UN High Commissioner for Refugees (2005-2015). He advocates strongly on climate change, sustainable development, and gender equality.

What are the most important scientific discoveries to know for competitive exams?

For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, state PSCs), the most frequently tested scientific discoveries include: Penicillin (Alexander Fleming, 1928), DNA structure (Watson and Crick, 1953), Theory of Relativity (Einstein, 1905), Radioactivity (Marie Curie, 1898), Telephone (Bell, 1876), WWW (Tim Berners-Lee, 1989), Laws of Motion and Gravity (Newton, 1687), Evolution (Darwin, 1859), Periodic Table (Mendeleev, 1869), X-rays (Roentgen, 1895), Vaccination (Jenner, 1796), and recent ones like CRISPR gene editing, gravitational waves detection (LIGO, 2015), and AlphaFold protein prediction (DeepMind, 2020). For Indian-specific science exams, also know Raman Effect (C.V. Raman, 1928) and contributions by J.C. Bose, Homi Bhabha, and Vikram Sarabhai.
