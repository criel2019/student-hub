IELTS Vocabulary List – 500 High-Frequency Academic Words

Grouped by topic for focused study. Every word includes its part of speech, meaning, and an example sentence. Ideal for IELTS Academic and General Training, Band 6.5–8+.

How to Use This List

Spaced Repetition

Study 20 new words per day. Review yesterday's words, last week's words, and last month's words each session. Apps like Anki make this systematic.

Learn in Context

Never learn words in isolation. For each word, study the example sentence, then write your own sentence. Reading IELTS practice essays reinforces context.

Word Families

Learn all forms: noun, verb, adjective, adverb. E.g., "ecology / ecological / ecologically / ecologist". This multiplies your vocabulary range efficiently.

Collocations Matter

Native speakers say "make progress" not "do progress". Learn words with their common partners. Collocations are tested heavily in Lexical Resource marking.

Topic Grouping

Focus on one topic at a time (e.g., all Environment words this week). This builds thematic clusters that are easier to recall during timed writing tasks.

Use in Writing Practice

Each week, write a 250-word practice essay using at least 10 words from your current topic list. This transforms passive knowledge into active skill.

Useful Academic Phrases for IELTS Writing

Expressing Opinions & Arguments

  • It is argued that... / It has been suggested that...
  • Evidence suggests that... / Research indicates that...
  • From a [economic / environmental / social] perspective,...
  • There is growing consensus that...
  • It is widely acknowledged that...
  • One could argue that... / Critics contend that...

Comparing and Contrasting

  • In contrast to... / Unlike... / Whereas...
  • Similarly, ... / By the same token,...
  • Despite [the fact that]... / Notwithstanding...
  • While [X] is true of [A], the opposite applies to [B].
  • The difference between X and Y lies in...

Cause and Effect

  • As a consequence of... / As a result of... / Due to...
  • This results in... / This leads to... / This gives rise to...
  • The primary cause of X is... / X can be attributed to...
  • Consequently, / Therefore, / Hence, / Thus,

Adding and Emphasising

  • In addition to... / Furthermore, / Moreover, / What is more,
  • Not only... but also... / Beyond this,
  • It is particularly noteworthy that...
  • Above all, / Most importantly,

IELTS Vocabulary Practice – 10 Gap-Fill Exercises

Fill in the blanks with the most appropriate word from the vocabulary list above.

  1. The government announced a series of ______ measures to protect coastal cities from rising sea levels. (mitigation / adaptation)
  2. Widespread ______ of rainforests in Southeast Asia has pushed hundreds of species to the brink of extinction. (deforestation / urbanisation)
  3. Social media ______ prioritise content that provokes emotional reactions, often amplifying misinformation. (algorithms / platforms)
  4. A well-designed ______ should develop students' intellectual curiosity alongside practical skills. (curriculum / pedagogy)
  5. Countries with high rates of adult ______ consistently outperform others on measures of democratic participation. (literacy / numeracy)
  6. The ______ of certain antibiotics has become a major public health concern as bacteria develop resistance. (overuse / epidemic)
  7. Economic ______ means that the children of wealthy families have far better educational outcomes than those from poorer backgrounds. (inequality / discrimination)
  8. The company implemented a remote working policy as part of its commitment to reducing its ______. (carbon footprint / ecological footprint)
  9. International ______ is essential for addressing challenges that no single nation can solve alone. (multilateralism / governance)
  10. High youth ______ rates in southern European nations reflect structural problems in the labour market. (unemployment / recession)

Advanced Sentence Starters for Band 7+ Writing

Opening an Essay with Impact

  • Few issues have generated as much debate in recent years as [topic].
  • The past decade has witnessed a dramatic [increase/decline/shift] in [topic].
  • At a time when [context], the question of [topic] demands urgent attention.
  • [Topic] lies at the heart of one of the most pressing debates of our time.
  • The relationship between [A] and [B] is far more complex than it might initially appear.

Introducing Counter-Arguments Elegantly

  • Admittedly, [concession], and this concern should not be dismissed lightly. However, [your counter-point].
  • While it would be naive to deny that [opposing view], the overall evidence points firmly towards [your position].
  • Critics of this position rightly point out that [counter-argument]. Nevertheless, [your response].
  • It is certainly true that [concession]. That said, [main point] remains compelling because [reason].

Describing Trends and Changes (for Task 1 crossover)

  • There has been a dramatic / steady / gradual / sharp rise in [topic].
  • [X] has increased exponentially / marginally / significantly over the past decade.
  • A noticeable downward trend can be observed in [topic] since [year].
  • The data reveal considerable fluctuation in [topic] between [year] and [year].
  • [Topic] has remained relatively stable / largely unchanged despite [factor].

Topic-Specific Writing Banks: Environment, Technology, Society

Use these ready-made ideas as starting points for IELTS essays. Combine topic vocabulary with these arguments for maximum band score impact.

TopicStrong "For" Argument + VocabularyStrong "Against" Argument + Vocabulary
Renewable energy replacing fossil fuels Solar and wind technology costs have plummeted, making renewable energy economically competitive. This transition reduces greenhouse gas emissions and addresses climate change while creating sustainable employment. The intermittency of renewable sources requires costly energy storage solutions. Developing nations with coal-dependent economies face severe economic disruption and unemployment during transition.
AI in healthcare Machine learning algorithms can analyse medical imaging with greater accuracy than human specialists, enabling earlier diagnosis and improving mortality rates for diseases such as cancer. Algorithmic bias in medical AI may perpetuate healthcare disparities for marginalised groups. The risk of over-reliance on automation could diminish the human empathy essential to good medical care.
Universal basic income As automation displaces workers, a universal income provides a safety net that enables social mobility and supports individuals during career transitions in a volatile labour market. The cost of fiscal policy to fund universal income would require significant tax increases, potentially stifling entrepreneurship and economic growth.
Social media age restrictions Restricting children's access to social media protects mental health, reduces cyberbullying, and limits exposure to misinformation during formative developmental years. Age restrictions are difficult to enforce and may drive usage underground. Young people also benefit from digital literacy and civic engagement opportunities that social media enables.
