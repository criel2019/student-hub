4. CET-4 Grammar Review

Subject-Verb Agreement

The verb must agree in number with its subject.

  • The number of students are increasing. вЖТ The number of students is increasing. ("The number" = singular)
  • Each of the participants were given a questionnaire. вЖТ Each of the participants was given a questionnaire.
  • Neither the students nor the teacher were aware. вЖТ Neither the students nor the teacher was aware. (verb agrees with nearer subject)

Tense Consistency

Maintain consistent tense within a paragraph unless there is a logical reason to shift.

  • She studied hard and passes the exam. вЖТ She studied hard and passed the exam.
  • The report shows that pollution had increased last year. вЖТ The report shows that pollution increased last year.

Conditional Sentences

  • Type 1 (possible future): If + Present Simple, will + VвВБ вАФ "If students study regularly, they will improve rapidly."
  • Type 2 (hypothetical present): If + Past Simple, would + VвВБ вАФ "If everyone recycled, we would significantly reduce landfill waste."
  • Type 3 (impossible past): If + Past Perfect, would have + VвВГ вАФ "If she had applied earlier, she would have received the scholarship."

Passive Voice

Widely used in academic and formal writing. Formed with "be + past participle".

  • Active: "Researchers conducted the study in 2023."
  • Passive: "The study was conducted in 2023."
  • Active: "The government will implement new policies."
  • Passive: "New policies will be implemented by the government."

Articles (a/an/the)

  • "a/an" вАФ first mention, one of many: "She is a teacher."
  • "the" вАФ specific, mentioned before, unique: "The teacher I mentioned is excellent."
  • No article вАФ plural or uncountable general nouns: "Water is essential for life." | "Students need motivation."

8. CET Reading Comprehension Skills

The Reading section (35% of score) includes Long Reading Comprehension and Information Matching. Here are the key skills and question types.

Question TypeStrategyCommon Pitfall
