1. Why Learn World Capitals & Currencies?

Knowledge of world capitals and currencies is tested in nearly every major competitive exam. Here is why this topic matters:

Exams Where This is Tested

  • UPSC Civil Services: World Geography + GS Paper 1 questions on international relations and geography
  • SSC CGL / CHSL / MTS: GK section consistently includes capitals and currencies
  • Banking (IBPS PO, SBI PO, RBI): Current affairs + static GK questions
  • Railway (RRB NTPC): GK section; often 3–5 questions per paper
  • State PCS Exams: All state-level civil service exams include world GK
  • Quiz Competitions (KBC, Olympiads): Capital/currency questions are standard
  • School Exams (Gaokao, A-levels): World geography is part of curriculum

Learning Strategy

How to Memorize Effectively

  • Learn by continent groups – do not try to memorize all 195 at once
  • Focus first on Asia, Europe, Africa (most tested)
  • Use mnemonics for similar-sounding capitals (e.g., Muscat=Oman, Manama=Bahrain)
  • Use flashcard apps (Anki, Quizlet) for spaced repetition
  • Solve previous year SSC/UPSC GK papers to identify patterns

Africa Notes for Exams

  • CFA Franc countries: 14 African countries (8 use XOF: West African CFA; 6 use XAF: Central African CFA). Both are pegged to the Euro.

8. Currency Trivia for Competitive Exams

Most Valuable Currency (Highest exchange rate vs USD)
Kuwaiti Dinar (KWD) – 1 KWD ≈ 3.26 USD. Kuwait's oil wealth makes its dinar the world's highest-valued currency unit.
World Reserve Currency
US Dollar (USD). Approximately 59% of global foreign exchange reserves are held in USD. Most international commodities (oil, gold) are priced in USD.
Second Most Valuable Currency
Bahraini Dinar (BHD) – 1 BHD ≈ 2.65 USD. Another Gulf nation with high oil revenues.
Euro – World's Second Reserve Currency
Euro (EUR) is the second most traded currency and held by ~20% of global reserves. It is used by 20 EU member states plus several non-EU countries.
Unique Currency Names
Lari (Georgia), Lev (Bulgaria), Tenge (Kazakhstan), Baht (Thailand), Dong (Vietnam), Kip (Laos), Kyat (Myanmar), Ngultrum (Bhutan), Rufiyaa (Maldives), Tögrög/Tugrik (Mongolia)
Countries that Switched to Euro (Recent)
Croatia switched to Euro on January 1, 2023 (from Kuna). Before Croatia: Lithuania (2015), Latvia (2014), Estonia (2011), Slovakia (2009), Slovenia (2007).
Currency of International Trade
US Dollar (USD). International oil trade (OPEC countries) is priced in USD. This gives USD its status as the "petrodollar." Gold is also priced in USD globally.
Oldest Currency Still in Use
British Pound Sterling (GBP) – over 1,200 years old, making it the world's oldest currency still in use. It is the 4th most traded currency globally.
Highest Inflation / Least Valuable Currency
Iranian Rial (IRR) – 1 USD ≈ 42,000 IRR. Venezuela, Zimbabwe and Iran have historically had extremely devalued currencies due to hyperinflation.
Countries Without Their Own Currency
Several countries use a foreign currency: Ecuador uses USD, Panama uses USD + Balboa, Timor-Leste uses USD. This is called "dollarization" or "currency substitution."
Most Traded Currencies (Forex Market)
1. USD (87.6%), 2. EUR (31.3%), 3. JPY (13.1%), 4. GBP (12.9%), 5. CNY (7.0%) – Note: percentages exceed 100% as each trade involves 2 currencies.
BRICS Nations and Their Currencies
Brazil (Real-BRL), Russia (Ruble-RUB), India (Rupee-INR), China (Yuan-CNY), South Africa (Rand-ZAR). Egypt, Ethiopia, UAE, Iran joined BRICS in 2024.

9. GK Quick Quiz – 15 Questions

Test your knowledge! Click "Show Answer" to reveal each answer.

What is the capital of Australia?
Canberra (NOT Sydney – a very common misconception tested in exams)
Which country has THREE capitals – Pretoria, Cape Town, and Bloemfontein?
South Africa. Pretoria = Executive capital, Cape Town = Legislative capital, Bloemfontein = Judicial capital.
What is the currency of Japan?
Japanese Yen (JPY). Tokyo is the capital of Japan.
Which is the world's most valuable currency (highest per-unit value against USD)?
Kuwaiti Dinar (KWD). 1 KWD ≈ 3.26 USD. Kuwait City is the capital of Kuwait.
Brazil moved its capital from which city to Brasília in 1960?
Rio de Janeiro. Brazil moved its capital to Brasília (a purpose-built city) in 1960.
What is the currency of Russia?
Russian Ruble (RUB). Moscow is the capital of Russia.
Which country has Naypyidaw as its capital, and what is its currency?
Myanmar (Burma). Currency: Myanmar Kyat (MMK). Capital was moved from Rangoon (Yangon) to Naypyidaw in 2006.
What is the capital of Canada?
Ottawa (NOT Toronto or Montreal – another common trick question). Currency: Canadian Dollar (CAD).
How many countries officially use the Euro as their currency (EU members only)?
20 EU member states as of 2023 (after Croatia joined the Eurozone on January 1, 2023).
Which African country has Abuja as its capital, and what is its currency?
Nigeria. Currency: Nigerian Naira (NGN). Capital was moved from Lagos to Abuja in 1991.
What is the administrative capital of Malaysia?
Putrajaya is the administrative/federal government capital. Kuala Lumpur is the official (national) capital and largest city. Currency: Malaysian Ringgit (MYR).
Which European country has Bern as its capital but is NOT part of the EU?
Switzerland. Capital: Bern. Currency: Swiss Franc (CHF). Switzerland is not an EU member and retains the Swiss Franc.
What is the currency of Saudi Arabia?
Saudi Riyal (SAR). Capital of Saudi Arabia: Riyadh.
Which two countries are "doubly landlocked" (completely surrounded by other landlocked countries)?
Uzbekistan (in Central Asia) and Liechtenstein (in Europe) are the only two doubly landlocked countries in the world.
What is the currency of China, and what is China's capital?
Currency: Chinese Yuan Renminbi (CNY), also called RMB. Capital: Beijing (also spelled Peking in older sources).

Exam Tip: Most Frequently Tested in SSC/UPSC/Banking

  • Capital of Australia = Canberra (NOT Sydney)
  • Capital of New Zealand = Wellington (NOT Auckland)
  • Capital of Canada = Ottawa (NOT Toronto)
  • Capital of Brazil = Brasília (NOT Rio de Janeiro)
  • Capital of Nigeria = Abuja (NOT Lagos)
  • South Africa has 3 capitals (Pretoria, Cape Town, Bloemfontein)
  • World's most valuable currency = Kuwaiti Dinar
  • Countries with 2 capitals: Bolivia, Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Netherlands, Tanzania
