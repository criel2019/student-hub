1. NEET Overview & Exam Pattern

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is India's single national-level entrance examination for admission to MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BSMS, BUMS, BHMS, and B.Sc Nursing courses in government and private medical colleges across India. It is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) once every year, typically in May.

Key Facts

Conducting BodyNational Testing Agency (NTA)
FrequencyOnce a year (typically May)
ModePen and Paper (OMR sheet)
Duration3 hours 20 minutes (200 minutes)
LanguageEnglish, Hindi + 11 regional languages
Total Seats (MBBS)~1,08,940 (government + private, 2024)
Total Applicants~24 lakh+ (2024)

What NEET Covers

NEET Admission Pathway

  • 15% All India Quota (AIQ): Merit-based from NEET score for all government medical colleges
  • 85% State Quota: Each state conducts its own counselling based on NEET rank
  • Deemed/Private Universities: Use NEET score; their own counselling
  • AIIMS & JIPMER: Now use NEET scores (since 2020)

Updated Pattern (Post-2021)

  • Since 2021, NEET has two sections per subject. Section B has 15 questions but you only attempt 10.
  • Maximum score is 720 (not 800) since 180 questions × 4 = 720.
  • Negative marking of −1 applies in Section B too – choose your 10 wisely.
  • The total is sometimes shown as "800 marks" in older references but the current maximum is 720.

3. NEET Syllabus Breakdown – Subject-wise Weightage

Most Important Fact About NEET Syllabus

  • NEET syllabus is strictly based on Class 11 and Class 12 NCERT textbooks for all three subjects.
  • Biology carries 360 marks (50% of total) – it is the deciding subject for most toppers.
  • The NMC (National Medical Commission) defines the NEET syllabus, and it follows NCERT closely.

4. Best Books for NEET Preparation

Golden Rule: NCERT First, Always

  • For Biology: 80–85% of NEET questions come directly from NCERT Class 11 & 12 textbooks.
  • For Chemistry: NCERT covers ~70% of NEET chemistry questions.
  • For Physics: NCERT theory + NCERT examples cover ~50% of NEET Physics.
  • Never use any reference book before completing NCERT thoroughly at least once.

5. 1-Year NEET Study Plan

This plan is designed for students who have completed Class 12 and are dedicating a full gap year to NEET preparation. For Class 12 students, compress and parallel-run the plan.

Month 1 – 3: Foundation Phase
Biology: Complete NCERT Class 11 (all 5 units) with deep reading. Make chapter-wise notes. Cover Diversity in Living World + Cell Structure + Plant Physiology.
Physics: Mechanics chapters (Kinematics through Work-Energy-Power). Solve all NCERT examples.
Chemistry: Physical Chemistry – Mole Concept, Atomic Structure, Chemical Bonding, Thermodynamics, Chemical Equilibrium, Ionic Equilibrium.
Target: Complete NCERT Bio Class 11 once + NCERT Chem Physical + 3 Physics chapters.
Month 4 – 6: Build-up Phase
Biology: NCERT Class 12 (Genetics, Reproduction, Biotechnology, Ecology). This is the most critical phase for Biology.
Physics: Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism, Optics. These form ~35% of NEET Physics.
Chemistry: Organic Chemistry basics – IUPAC, Isomerism, GOC (General Organic Chemistry), Hydrocarbons.
Target: Complete NCERT Bio Class 12 once. Start solving MTG Fingertips Bio chapter-wise.
Mock Tests: Start taking chapter-based tests (1 per week).
Month 7 – 9: Completion & Practice Phase
Biology: Complete remaining chapters. Begin second reading of NCERT Bio (both classes). Focus on diagrams – label every diagram from NCERT.
Physics: Thermodynamics, Modern Physics, Waves & SHM, Properties of Matter.
Chemistry: Organic reactions (Haloalkanes, Alcohols, Aldehydes, Amines). Inorganic: p-block elements, coordination compounds.
Mock Tests: 2 full-length NEET mock tests per week. Analyze for 2 hours after each test.
Target: Score 450+ in mocks consistently.
Month 10 – 12: Intensive Revision Phase
Biology: NCERT-only revision – 3rd and 4th reading. Focus on NCERT lines, diagrams, examples, and page-specific facts. Solve last 10 years' NEET Bio PYQ.
Physics: Formula sheet revision + solve previous 5 years' NEET Physics.
Chemistry: Inorganic NCERT revision (s,p,d,f blocks), organic named reactions list, physical chemistry formula sheet.
Mock Tests: Daily 1 full mock or 1 subject-wise test.
Target: Score 580+ in full mocks. Identify and eliminate all weak areas.
Final 2 Weeks (Pre-Exam)
No new topics. Only revision of notes, formula sheets, NCERT diagrams, and important lines.
Solve 1 mock per day in actual exam conditions (3 hrs 20 min, OMR marking).
Sleep schedule normalization – sleep by 10 PM, wake by 6 AM.
Light physical activity daily. Avoid social media and exam anxiety triggers.

6. Biology: Most Important NCERT Lines for NEET

NEET Biology is famous for testing specific lines, facts, and even page numbers from NCERT. Here are the most frequently tested NCERT statements:

Biological Classification & Diversity

"Viruses are obligate intracellular parasites." – (Biological Classification)
"Chrysophytes, Dinoflagellates, Euglenoids, Slime Moulds and Protozoans are all placed under Kingdom Protista." – (Biological Classification)
"Cyanobacteria are also referred to as blue-green algae." – (Kingdom Monera)
"Lichens are symbiotic associations between algae (phycobiont) and fungi (mycobiont)." – (Plant Kingdom)

Cell Structure & Biomolecules

"The bond between two amino acids is a peptide bond." – (Biomolecules)
"All living organisms are made of cells." – (Cell: Unit of Life)
"The fluid mosaic model of plasma membrane was proposed by Singer and Nicolson in 1972." – (Cell: Unit of Life)
"Ribosomes are the site of protein synthesis." – (Cell: Unit of Life)
"The middle lamella is composed mainly of calcium pectate." – (Cell Wall)
"Enzymes are primarily proteins. However, RNA molecules can also act as enzymes (ribozymes)." – (Biomolecules)

Plant Physiology

"Transpiration is a necessary evil." – (Transport in Plants)
"The site of photosynthesis is the chloroplast." – (Photosynthesis)
"Photorespiration does not produce ATP or NADPH." – (Photosynthesis)
"C4 plants lack photorespiration." – (Photosynthesis)
"Kranz anatomy is characteristic of C4 plants." – (Photosynthesis)

Human Physiology

"The heart is a myogenic organ." – (Body Fluids and Circulation)
"Insulin is a peptide hormone." – (Chemical Coordination and Integration)
"The liver is the largest gland in the body." – (Digestion and Absorption)
"Surfactant is secreted by Type II pneumocytes (alveolar cells)." – (Breathing and Exchange)
"The functional unit of kidney is nephron." – (Excretory Products)
"Loop of Henle is the part of nephron present in the medulla of kidney." – (Excretory Products)

Genetics & Evolution

"DNA is the genetic material in most organisms; RNA in some viruses." – (Molecular Basis of Inheritance)
"Griffith's experiment (1928) first demonstrated transformation in bacteria." – (Molecular Basis)
"Hershey and Chase (1952) proved DNA is the genetic material using bacteriophage." – (Molecular Basis)
"Watson and Crick (1953) proposed the double helix model of DNA." – (Molecular Basis)
"The central dogma states: DNA → RNA → Protein." – (Molecular Basis)
"Darwin's finches are classic examples of adaptive radiation." – (Evolution)
"Hardy-Weinberg principle states that allele frequencies in a population remain constant unless acted upon by evolutionary forces." – (Evolution)

7. Subject-wise Preparation Strategy

Biology Strategy (Most Important)

The NCERT-First, NCERT-Last Rule

  • Read NCERT Biology at least 4–5 times before the exam. Each reading reveals new facts you missed earlier.
  • Every diagram in NCERT is important – be able to label every part of every diagram.
  • Focus especially on: Cell organelles, Mendel's laws, DNA replication, transcription, translation diagrams.
  • Genetics numericals (dihybrid crosses, pedigree charts) are guaranteed in every NEET – practice all NCERT examples.
  • Evolution chapter: memorize all the experiments and their scientists (Redi, Pasteur, Miller, Urey, Watson, Crick).

Physics Strategy

  • NEET Physics is application-based, not derivation-based. Focus on using formulas correctly rather than deriving them.
  • Allocate 45–50 minutes for Physics in the exam. Do not spend more – Biology needs your time.
  • Modern Physics (Photoelectric, Bohr's model, Nuclear) is formula-based and easy to score – never skip this unit.
  • Ray optics and wave optics are both tested. Know the difference between interference (YDSE) and diffraction conditions.
  • Semiconductor questions in NEET are conceptual – understand n-type, p-type, p-n junction, and logic gates.
  • Attempt Physics questions in a calculated manner – NEET Physics is not as lengthy as JEE Physics, but careless errors waste marks.

Chemistry Strategy

  • Inorganic Chemistry (NCERT ONLY): Read and re-read the s-block, p-block, d-block chapters. All reactions, oxides, and properties asked in NEET are from NCERT tables and paragraphs.
  • Organic Chemistry: IUPAC naming, isomerism (structural + stereo), named reactions (Markovnikov, Aldol, Cannizzaro, Hoffmann, etc.) – make a one-page named reactions cheat sheet.
  • Physical Chemistry: Master these numericals: mole concept, molarity/molality, equilibrium constants (Kp, Kc), rate laws, Nernst equation, Gibbs free energy calculations.
  • Coordination chemistry (Class 12) typically gives 2–3 questions – memorize IUPAC names of complexes and types of isomerism.
  • Biomolecules and polymers chapter: memorize names, monomers, types. These are high-frequency, easy MCQs.

8. Exam Day Strategy & Time Allocation

NEET Exam Day Timeline (200 minutes total)

  • Arrive at center 60 minutes before reporting time.
  • Carry: Admit card (printed), Passport size photo, valid government ID (Aadhaar/Passport).
  • No electronic devices, pencil boxes, or wallets inside. Only a transparent pen pouch is allowed.
  • OMR sheet is very sensitive – be careful when marking; erasures are not valid.

Recommended Time Allocation Inside the Exam

5–50 min (45 min)Botany – Section A & B45 Qs (attempt 45)Start with the subject you're strongest in – Biology. Attempt all section A; for B, skip genuinely unknown.

185–200 min (15 min)Review all sections–Revisit marked questions. NEVER change a confident answer. Only correct clear errors.

OMR Strategy

  • Use only the permitted blue/black ballpoint pen for OMR marking.
  • Fill the bubble completely and darkly – partial bubbling may cause rejection.
  • Rough work must be done on the rough sheets provided, never on the OMR sheet.
  • Mark on OMR sheet simultaneously as you solve – do not wait to mark at end (risk of running out of time).
  • For questions you are skipping temporarily, mark in the question booklet only; transfer to OMR when you return.

9. Common Mistakes to Avoid in NEET Preparation

Critical Mistakes that Cost Marks

  • Using reference books before NCERT: Students who jump to Trueman's or Pradeep without first mastering NCERT have weak foundations and score lower.
  • Not reading NCERT multiple times: One reading is never enough. Top NEET scorers read NCERT Biology 5–7 times. New facts surface every time.
  • Ignoring NCERT Inorganic Chemistry: Many students skip NCERT and try to memorize from notes. NEET specifically tests NCERT phrasing in Inorganic.
  • Treating Biology as secondary: Biology is 50% of NEET. A student who scores 340/360 in Biology and 180/360 in PCM will outperform someone who does the opposite.
  • Not labeling diagrams: NEET asks for labeled diagram identification regularly. If you cannot label TS of dicot stem or mitosis phases, marks are lost.
  • No previous year paper practice: NEET frequently repeats concepts (sometimes exact questions) from past years. Solving 10 years' papers is mandatory.
  • Overconfidence in Physics formulas: NEET Physics tests concept application. Knowing formulas without understanding when to apply them leads to errors.
  • Skipping Ecology: Ecology (Biodiversity, Ecosystem, Populations) gives 8–10 easy questions per year. Students who skip it lose guaranteed marks.
  • Poor time management: Spending 80 minutes on Physics and 40 on Biology is the opposite of what you should do for maximum score.
  • Studying under stress without breaks: Burnout is real in a 1-year NEET preparation cycle. Schedule mandatory recreation and social time weekly.

10. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. How many times should I read NCERT for NEET Biology?
Most NEET toppers read NCERT Biology at least 5–7 times. The first reading is for understanding; the second for retention; subsequent readings reveal facts you missed. Each reading should be more focused – by the 4th reading, you should be able to recall information from specific NCERT paragraphs. Use sticky notes or margin annotations to mark important lines. The MTG Fingertips Biology book cross-references each question to its NCERT source, which helps identify which paragraphs are tested most frequently.
Q2. Is one year enough to prepare for NEET?
Yes – one year of focused, disciplined preparation is sufficient to score 550+ in NEET, which qualifies for good government medical colleges in most states. Students aiming for AIIMS (top 100 AIR) typically need 2–3 years of preparation starting from Class 11. For a gap year student with good PCB background from school, 1 year with 8–10 hours of daily study and proper mock test practice is a realistic timeline to score 600+. The key variables are quality of preparation (not just hours), consistency, and continuous mock test analysis.
Q3. Which subject should I focus on most for NEET?
Biology is undisputedly the most important subject for NEET – it carries 360 marks (50% of total). More importantly, Biology has the highest ROI (return on investment) per hour studied because NEET Biology questions are directly from NCERT and highly predictable. Chemistry has the second-best ROI because Physical Chemistry is formula-based and Inorganic is NCERT-based. Physics is the most time-consuming per question but can be managed with strong formulae knowledge. Recommended time ratio: Biology 40% : Chemistry 30% : Physics 30%.
Q4. What is the difference between NEET and AIIMS entrance exam?
Since 2020, AIIMS no longer conducts its own separate entrance examination. All 19 AIIMS institutes (including AIIMS Delhi, AIIMS Mumbai, AIIMS Jodhpur, etc.) admit students based on NEET scores and NEET All India Ranks. Similarly, JIPMER Puducherry also shifted to NEET-based admissions. So there is effectively only one exam to prepare for – NEET. The difference is that admission to AIIMS Delhi requires being in the top 50 ranks in NEET (approximately 710+ score), making it extraordinarily competitive.
Q5. How should I manage anxiety and stress during NEET preparation?
NEET anxiety is real and must be actively managed. Proven strategies: (1) Take 1 full day off per week – complete rest from studies; (2) Regular physical exercise (30 minutes daily – even walking reduces cortisol levels significantly); (3) Meditation or deep breathing (even 10 minutes daily); (4) Mock tests desensitize exam anxiety – the more full-length mocks you take under real conditions, the less anxious you feel on actual exam day; (5) Avoid comparing your progress with peers; (6) Celebrate small wins – completing a chapter, solving a difficult problem; (7) Talk to a counselor or mentor if anxiety becomes severe. Mental health directly impacts study efficiency – a student studying 8 focused hours in a calm state outperforms one studying 12 stressed hours.
