❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common student questions

What is JEE Main and how is it different from JEE Advanced? +

JEE Main (Joint Entrance Examination Main) is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) twice a year (January and April). It is the qualifying exam for admission to NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs. It is also the gateway to JEE Advanced.

JEE Advanced is conducted by the IITs for admission to all 23 IITs. Only the top 2.5 lakh JEE Main qualifiers are eligible. JEE Advanced is significantly harder — it tests deep conceptual understanding through multi-concept, application-based problems. The marks in JEE Main do not carry forward to JEE Advanced; they are separate exams with separate scores.

How should I prepare for NEET in 1 year? +

A proven 1-year NEET strategy:

  • Months 1–4 (Foundation): Complete all NCERT Biology (Class 11 + 12) — this alone covers 80–85% of NEET Biology. Do NCERT Physics and Chemistry in parallel.
  • Months 5–8 (Practice): Solve 100+ MCQs daily. Focus on weak chapters. Use reference books like H.C. Verma for Physics, O.P. Tandon for Chemistry.
  • Months 9–11 (Revision): Revise with flashcards, diagrams, and quick notes. Solve 10 previous year papers (2015–2025).
  • Month 12 (Mock Tests): Take 3 full-length mocks per week. Analyse each mock for timing and accuracy. Do not study new topics.

Biology (360 marks) is the scoring section. Prioritize it. A score of 600+ is achievable with consistent effort.

What is Gaokao and how do Chinese students prepare for it? +

Gaokao (高考) is China's National College Entrance Examination, held each June. It is taken by approximately 13 million students annually and is one of the most competitive exams in the world. The total score is 750 (some provinces have 680).

Core subjects: Chinese (150 marks), Mathematics (150 marks), Foreign Language / English (150 marks), plus 3 elective subjects (300 marks total) from Sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) or Humanities (History, Geography, Political Science).

How students prepare: Most Chinese students spend 3 years in senior high school (高中) preparing, with 10–12 hours of study per day in the final year. Practice exams (模拟题) from previous years are the backbone of preparation. Top universities like Peking University and Tsinghua require scores of 700+/750.

How to get a scholarship to study abroad from India or China? +

Top fully-funded scholarships for Indian students:

  • Chevening Scholarship — UK Government, for master's degrees. Deadline: November each year.
  • Fulbright-Nehru Fellowship — US Department of State, for research and postgrad study in the USA.
  • DAAD Scholarship — German Academic Exchange Service, for studying in Germany (often tuition-free).
  • Commonwealth Scholarship — For postgrad study in the UK and other Commonwealth nations.
  • Inlaks Foundation — For Indian students studying at top global universities.

For Chinese students: CSC Scholarship (Chinese Government Scholarship), Belt and Road Initiative Scholarship, and institutional scholarships from partner universities.

General tips: maintain a GPA above 3.5, get strong letters of recommendation, write a compelling Statement of Purpose (SOP), and apply 12–18 months early.

What is the best way to learn Python programming from scratch? +

A structured path to learn Python from zero:

  1. Week 1–2 (Basics): Variables, data types, loops, conditionals, functions. Use Python's official tutorial or CS50P (free on edX).
  2. Week 3–4 (Intermediate): Lists, dictionaries, file I/O, error handling, modules, OOP (classes & objects).
  3. Month 2 (Projects): Build a calculator, a to-do app, a basic web scraper. Projects solidify learning far better than passive reading.
  4. Month 3+ (Specialization): Choose your path — Data Science (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib), Web Dev (Django/Flask), or Automation (Selenium, BeautifulSoup).
  5. Practice daily: Solve at least 2 problems daily on LeetCode or HackerRank starting from Easy level.

For competitive programming (ICPC, CodeChef), also learn C++ — it is faster and more widely used in contests.

What IELTS score is required for universities in USA, UK, Canada, and Australia? +

Minimum IELTS overall band scores required by country (for undergraduate/postgraduate admission):

  • USA: Most universities require 6.5–7.0 overall. Top universities (MIT, Harvard, Stanford) prefer 7.5+.
  • UK: Most Russell Group universities require 6.5–7.0 overall with no band below 6.0. Oxford and Cambridge: 7.0–7.5.
  • Canada: Most universities require 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0. For PR (Express Entry): CLB 7 = IELTS 6.0 in each band.
  • Australia: Undergraduate: 6.0–6.5. Postgraduate: 6.5–7.0. Nursing/Medicine: 7.0+ with no band below 7.0.

Tips for achieving Band 7+: practise writing 2 essays per week, read BBC/Guardian/The Hindu daily, and shadow TED Talk speakers for pronunciation. A score of 7.5+ is achievable in 2–3 months of focused preparation.
